Company Story

Who Cape & Cart is, and why this campaign mattered

Cape & Cart is an anonymised portfolio scenario for a South African online retailer built around practical home, lifestyle, and everyday essentials. The story gives the analytics work a real business context: a growing brand, a stretched marketing budget, and a team that needed proof before moving spend.

Cape & Cart started as a Cape Town marketplace for locally sourced home and lifestyle products. By 2024 it had grown into a national ecommerce retailer, shipping across South Africa and competing with bigger players on convenience, trust, and curated ranges rather than endless inventory.

The H1 growth campaign was meant to do two things at once: defend profitable Search demand while using YouTube and display to introduce the brand to new shoppers before peak retail season. The problem was not a lack of marketing activity. The problem was that every platform told a different version of the truth.

This analytics platform was built for the moment Cape & Cart's leadership asked a simple question with expensive consequences: which channel deserves the next rand?

Executive Decision Brief

What I would do Monday morning for the H1 campaign

The dashboard has one job: turn six months of messy ecommerce marketing data into budget decisions a team can defend. These are the actions the evidence supports now.

Move 1
Protect Search, but stop letting it take all the credit

4.2x ROAS deserves scale, but last-click attribution is probably absorbing demand YouTube created earlier in the journey.

Move 2
Treat YouTube as an attribution investigation

2.9x ROAS is below target on paper. Add view-through conversions before cutting awareness spend.

Move 3
Turn checkout abandonment into a remarketing audience

16.8K add-to-cart users did not purchase. That is not a vanity metric; it is the warmest recovery pool in the data.

Channel Analysis

Where did the H1 campaign spend R1.483M — and what is attribution hiding?

Across 1 Jan-30 Jun 2024, blended ROAS of 3.8× beats the 3.0× target. But the real value is not the headline number. The split exposes two jobs: Search captures ecommerce demand at 4.2×, while YouTube creates demand that last-click reporting may be handing to Search later.

01
The decision is not simply scale winners and cut losers

YouTube at 2.9× is marginally below target — but YouTube's role is upper-funnel brand awareness, not last-click conversion. Comparing it to Search on ROAS alone misrepresents its value. It contributes 1.89M impressions (15.6% of total) at a low CPM — view-through attribution would show a stronger contribution.

DV360 is your biggest impression source but weakest converter

DV360 delivers 4.21M impressions — 34.8% of total — but converts at only 0.8% per click, the lowest across all channels. It's not a budget problem (R278K is appropriate for display). It's an audience targeting or creative problem. Recommendation: tighten audience segments, exclude low-intent placements, and A/B test creatives before scaling this channel.

Pipeline Reliability

The part nobody sees: stopping bad campaign data before it becomes strategy

The numbers above are reliable because the pipeline behaves like a skeptical analyst. Bronze preserves raw evidence, including dirty ecommerce product and order exports; validation refuses suspicious rows; dbt/Silver normalises the metrics; and Gold becomes the only place ROAS and product performance are allowed to exist.

📥
BRONZE
Scenario-scale ingestion from 5 sources via BQ Data Transfer. All columns STRING — zero rows rejected. Mixed dates, currency variants, and missing fields land exactly as received.
523,480 modelled rows · marketing_bronze.* · daily append

BEAM VALIDATION
Apache Beam ValidateAndTagRow DoFn routes each row: valid to Silver, bad to quarantine. 31,409 rows flagged — mostly YouTube date format issues and Ads missing currency.

dbt staging models type-cast, deduplicate, and normalise. Cost micros ÷ 1,000,000. Currency uppercased and trimmed. Incremental merge on unique_key prevents double-counting.
492,071 rows · 5 dbt models · 18 tests all passing
📊

fct_cross_channel_performance: one row per channel per day. Single source of truth for all ROAS, CTR, and conversion metrics. Full overwrite — always idempotent.
marketing_gold.* · Looker Studio · Airflow 04:00 UTC daily
💡
Why the pipeline design matters for the numbers above

Without the Beam validation step, cost micros from Google Ads would inflate spend figures by 1,000,000×. A raw cost_micros value of 338,138,553 looks like R338M — it's actually R338.14. This is the most common Google Ads data engineering mistake. The dbt Silver model normalises it before any ROAS is calculated, ensuring every number in this dashboard is correct.
